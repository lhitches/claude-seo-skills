# Topical Authority Map (free skill)

Build a 4-cluster Topical Authority Map from any seed keyword. Five tabs of strategic output, sequenced across 12 weeks, with content briefs ready to hand to a writer.

This is the workflow we run inside StudioHawk on every client. We are giving it to you free because the strategy is the part the tool was never going to do for you.

---

## What you get

When you run this skill on a seed keyword (for example "premium Australian furniture"), Claude produces:

1. **A spreadsheet (.xlsx) with five tabs:**
   - **Core Pages.** 12 to 20 commercial pages, the money pages users buy from.
   - **AOR Pages.** 60 to 80 supporting authority articles, organised by sub-topic.
   - **Linking Map.** Which page links to which page, with what anchor text, in what direction.
   - **Buyer Journey.** Every page tagged TOFU, MOFU, or BOFU.
   - **Publishing Order.** 90 articles sequenced across 12 weeks, with content briefs for the first 30.

2. **A strategy document (.md):**
   - Central entity definition (the one thing your site is allowed to be known for).
   - Source context and persona.
   - Folder structure rationale.
   - Linking rules.
   - Buyer journey gaps and how to fill them.
   - Publishing schedule (Avalanche Theory).
   - Information gain opportunities.

You hand the spreadsheet to your team and the strategy doc to your editor. By Monday they are shipping the first article in the AOR wave.

---

## Why four clusters, not one

Most SEO tools sell you a topical map. The map they sell you is a keyword cluster. That is one quarter of what you actually need. The other three layers are where the compounding actually happens.

- **Core** is the destination, not the strategy. Sales pages alone do not rank.
- **AOR** is the road. Supporting authority articles that route satisfaction signals into the core pages.
- **Linking** is what turns a pile of articles into a structure Google can read as a topical authority map.
- **Buyer Journey** tags every page against where the customer is in their decision (TOFU researching, MOFU comparing, BOFU buying). Most websites have a giant MOFU gap. That is where conversions hide.

Add Publishing Order and you have a 12-week plan, not just a content list.

## Quick start

1. Install (see INSTALL.md).
2. Open Claude Code or Claude Desktop.
3. Type: `Build a topical authority map for premium Australian furniture` (or your own seed keyword).
4. Answer Claude's questions at each of the five checkpoints (central entity, source context plus persona, attribute list, merge and split decisions, buyer journey gaps).
5. Claude generates the spreadsheet and strategy document.
6. Open the spreadsheet. Read in this order: Core, AOR, Linking, Buyer Journey, Publishing Order.
7. Hand the first 30 content briefs to your writer. Start shipping the AOR wave.
8. Do not publish a single core page edit until at least the first wave of AOR is live (weeks 1 to 6).

The compounding effect kicks in around week 6, accelerates through week 10, and lands fully on the Core pages by week 12.
